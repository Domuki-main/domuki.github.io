A symphony is an extended musical composition in Western classical music, most often written by composers for orchestra.

1. Position

2. Classification

A Symphony Orchestra is defined as a large ensemble composed of:

  1. wind instruments (木管)
  2. string instruments (弦乐)
  3. brass instruments (铜管)
  4. percussion instruments (打击)

and organized to perform classical music.

String instruments include harp, violin, viola, cello, and double bass.

Wind instruments include flute, oboe, clarinet and bassoons.

Brass instruments include French horn, trumpet, trombone and tuba.

Percussion instruments include timpani, snare drum, bass drum, cymbals, triangle, celesta and piano.

2. Instrument

1. String instruments

  1. Violin

The lowest note of violin is “SOL” as the picture showing.

The high note rely on the ability of player.

For this, you can refer Bach: Violin Partita No.3 in E major, BWV 1006

  1. Viola

The lowest note of viola is “DO” as the picture showing.

The high note rely on the ability of player. But its mid&bass warmer than violin.

For this, you can refer Bach: Cello Suite No.5 in C minor, BWV 1011

  1. Cello

The lowest note of cello is “DO” as the picture showing, One octave lower than viola.

The bass of Cello is useful but its mid bass also can use in solo.

For this, you can refer Saint-Saens: Le Cygne
