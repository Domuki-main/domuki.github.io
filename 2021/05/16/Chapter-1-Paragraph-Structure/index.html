Chapter 1: Paragraph Structure

Author: Domuki Date: May 16, 2021  11:28:10 Category: Academic Writing

Parts of a Paragraph

Paragraphs normally contain 3 parts:

  1. topic sentence
  2. supporting sentences
  3. concluding sentence (most paragraph have)

The Topic Sentence

the most important in a paragraph

Here are 3 important points about TS:

  1. a TS is a complete sentence

  2. a TS contains 2 parts: a topic and a controlling idea.

    Improving online social networks(topic) can help professionals communicate.(controlling idea)

  3. a TS is the most general statement in the paragraph and it doesn’t give any specific details. (but cannot be too general, it needs to show what the paragraph will be about)

The Position of Topic Sentences

the TS usually is the first sentence in a paragraph.

Experienced writers sometimes put TS in other locations; however, the best spot is usually right at the beginning.

At times, a TS may come at the end of the paragraph. In this case, the paragraph might begin with a series of examples or facts.

then the TS at end would be the conclusion drawn from these examples or facts.

Supporting Sentences

Supporting sentences explain or prove the topic sentence.

There are many kinds of specific supporting details such as examples, facts and statistics, and quotations.

Different between Opinions and Facts

  1. opinion: Photographs of ultrathin fashion models send the wrong message to girls and young women.
  2. fact but need to be proofed: Fashion models are extremely thin.
  3. specific supporting detail: The average model weighs 23 percent less than the average woman of the same height.

Concluding Sentence

its function just as its name

CS serves 2 purposes:

  1. it signal the end of paragraph, and remind reader the important idea.
  2. it can do the summarizing (paraphrasing the topic sentence).

End-of-Paragraph Signals

with comma without comma
In brief, the evidence suggests that…
Lastly, there can be no doubt that …
In conclusion, These examples show that…
Therefore, we can see that…

The Writing Process

  1. Prewrite to get ideas (listing)

    to choose a topic and generate ideas

  2. Organize your ideas

    to decide which ideas to use and how you will organize them

  3. Write the first draft

    to write a first draft following by the plan

  4. Revise the draft

    to change what you have written to improve it by checking the content and organization. it is helpful to have another person’s perspective on your writing (peer reviewer)

  5. Edit and proofread the draft

    to check for possible mistakes in grammar, punctuation, spelling, and sentence structure.

  6. Write a new draft

    continuous writing, revising, and rewriting until you are satisfied with the final product
